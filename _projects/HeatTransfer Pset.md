In my Heat Transfer class I learned a significant amount from our fourth problem set. I learned how a photovoltaic panel works, and how to analyze its thermal resistance network. Thus, I was able to deduce temperature at different points along the assembly: glass top surface and silicon layer. Within this same problem I calculated solar to electricity conversion efficiency and resulting electricity power flux produced by the PV panel. Additionally, on the third problem, given minimal information I was able to deduce the length of a thermal pin, thermal resistance, and fin effectiveness. This required extensive algebra and MATLAB coding. The entirety of this problem set will be useful to me in the future because now I have the heat transfer knowledge set to tackle any problem, using all tools from algebra to MATLAB.
